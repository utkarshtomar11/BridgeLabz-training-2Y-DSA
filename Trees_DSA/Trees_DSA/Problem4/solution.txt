================================================================
Problem 4: BST Operations - SOLUTION
================================================================

Initial BST:
              15
             /  \
            10   20
           / \   / \
          8  12 17  25

----------------------------------------------------------------
a) Delete node 10 (has two children: 8 and 12):
----------------------------------------------------------------
   Node 10 has two children -> use INORDER SUCCESSOR method

   Step 1: Find inorder successor of 10
           = smallest node in RIGHT subtree of 10
           = 12 (only node in right subtree)

   Step 2: Replace value of node 10 with 12

   Step 3: Delete original node 12 (it's a leaf -> simple delete)

   BST after deleting 10:
              15
             /  \
            12   20
           /    / \
          8    17  25

----------------------------------------------------------------
b) Insert roll number 14 into the modified tree:
----------------------------------------------------------------
   Step 1: 14 < 15 -> go LEFT  (to 12)
   Step 2: 14 > 12 -> go RIGHT of 12
   Step 3: Right of 12 is NULL -> INSERT 14 here

   BST after inserting 14:
              15
             /  \
            12   20
           /  \  / \
          8   14 17  25

----------------------------------------------------------------
c) Insert roll number 9:
----------------------------------------------------------------
   Step 1: 9 < 15 -> go LEFT  (to 12)
   Step 2: 9 < 12 -> go LEFT  (to 8)
   Step 3: 9 > 8  -> go RIGHT of 8
   Step 4: Right of 8 is NULL -> INSERT 9 here

   BST after inserting 9:
              15
             /  \
            12   20
           /  \  / \
          8   14 17  25
           \
            9

----------------------------------------------------------------
d) Finding roll numbers between 10 and 20:
----------------------------------------------------------------
   --> Use INORDER traversal

   Reason: Inorder visits nodes in sorted ascending order.
   Traverse the tree and collect nodes where value >= 10
   AND value <= 20. You can prune branches early:
   - If current node < 10, skip left subtree
   - If current node > 20, skip right subtree
   This is more efficient than checking every node.

   Result from current tree: 12, 14, 15, 17

----------------------------------------------------------------
e) Time complexity for searching roll number 25:
----------------------------------------------------------------

   Best Case (Balanced BST):
   Height = O(log n), here log2(7) ≈ 3
   Search path: 15 -> 20 -> 25  (3 comparisons)
   --> Time Complexity: O(log n)

   Worst Case (Completely Skewed BST):
   If tree degrades to a linked list (sorted insertion),
   every node must be visited one by one.
   --> Time Complexity: O(n)

   In our current tree:
   15 -> 20 -> 25 = 3 comparisons = O(log n) ✓
